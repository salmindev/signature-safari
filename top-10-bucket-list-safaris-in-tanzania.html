Top 10 bucket-list Safaris in Tanzania

Where To Travel And What To Do During Your Holiday Stay In Tanzania In 2024 – Signature Safari?

Tanzania country is filled with great and many attractions and once in a lifetime experience, making it really hard to narrow down your list of “must-do” on your holiday! If you the kind of traveler that tries to get in as much as possible in your trip in a short time this one is for you! To make sure you don’t miss out on the big 10 in Tanzania, Signature Safari put together the ultimate travel bucket list for you enjoy in Tanzania Safari in 2024! You can use this checklist as an inspiration for places to visit, things to do, and activities to add to your trip when booking your trip to Tanzania, you can also contact Signature Safari for more consultation.

Top 10 things you “must” do and see in Tanzania:

  • Serengeti National Park

    The great wildebeest crossing the Mara River: With a great chance of seeing all the Big 5 and lots of other wild African animals in their natural habitat it’s no surprise that the Serengeti National Park is seen as the ideal safari destination for many tourist and travelers around the world.In the right season (usually between July to October )you can see the famous wildebeest migration, the annual migration of millions of wildebeest and zebra, well-known from the river crossings from Tanzania’s Serengeti to Kenya’s Maasai Mara go and return searching for food and pastures.

    See More about the Serengeti National Park

  • Climbing to the Top of Mount Kilimanjaro

    Standing at the crater rim of the highest mount in Africa the Kilimanjaro: Climbing to the summit of Africa’s highest mountain and enjoy sunrise with a view from the crater rim. The most scenic route is called Lemosho route and Machame route. Approximately 5-9 days of your trip for reaching the top and return. There is different routes, with different number of days and different budget for this climbing, you can contact Signature Safari to be sure of this and more consultation.

    See More about the Mount Kilimanjaro National Park

  • Zanzibar Archipelago /Zanzibar Island

    Colorful fish and beautiful corals underwater: Tanzania has world-class diving, and the Zanzibar Archipelago is one of the top locations for diving in Tanzania. Excellent and Amazing snorkeling options are also available. Find out where to go in the top 10 dive sites in the Zanzibar Archipelago.

    See More about Zanzibar

  • Explore the Natural zoo of the Ngorongoro Crater

    The Ngorongoro Conservation Area: The Ngorongoro Crater is the world’s largest inactive, intact, and un-flooded crater and it is full of animals. The walls of the crater are around 600m high, and peering down into the crater for the first time is jaw-dropping beautiful.

    See More about Ngorongoro Conservation Area

  • Take a Hot Air Balloon Ride at sunrise over the plains of the Serengeti National Park

    Hot air balloon at Serengeti National Park Take to the skies and see the animals from above as the sun rises over the endless plains of the Legendary Serengeti National Park. Finish with a champagne and breakfast and we are talking about an experience you will never forget for sure we have already done 2 Balloon Safari in Serengeti and it’s not even the mid of January 2023! Why don’t you try? Contact with Signature Safari for more details and booking requests.See the picture of our 4th January 2023 clients who took off with Balloon Safari in central Serengeti National Park.

  • Explore some of Tanzania’s lesser known National Parks but the best place to be

    There are many other national parks and game reserve in Tanzania to visit after you have seen the Serengeti National Park, especially if you have enough days. Each of them unique in their own way. The top National Parks to visits in Tanzania are:

    • Tarangire National Park
    • Lake Manyara National Park
    • Ruaha National Park
    • The Selous Game Reserve / Nyerere National Park
    • Katavi National Park
    • Kitulo National Park
    • Mikumi National Park

  • Swim with Whale & Sharks

    Despite their name, these gentle giants are not sharks. They are the world’s largest fish and can grow up to 20m and longer. From October to March you can hop on a boat and go snorkeling with them. Head to Mafia Island; for this truly incredible experience of a lifetime. Contact with signature for early booking since it has a limit for clients in every month.

  • Visit the Dr.Jane Goodall Chimpanzees in Gombe National Park

    Chimpanzee at Gombe National Park: The chimpanzees home in,Gombe National Park were made famous by Dr Jane Goodall who has been doing her research here for decades now. Your other option is to visit the chimpanzees in Mahale National Park, both national parks are located in the west of Tanzania on the gorgeous Lake Tanganyika. An excellent place to swim and snorkel when you are not visiting the chimpanzees.

  • Visit one of Tanzania’s Stunning Beaches

    Zanzibar Beach: There are plenty of options to go to the beach in Tanzania. The Zanzibar Archipelago offers beaches for everyone, but there are also: Other islands – like private island Fanjove or the Songo Songo Archipelago in Kilwa district. Inland lakes – like Lake Tanganyika and Lake Victoria, and the long east coast of Tanzania itself. Thinking of white sand, palm trees and crystal clear, blue waters. Accommodation from backpacking options to super luxurious means there is a slice of paradise to be found for every budget in Tanzania.

    See More about Zanzibar

  • Wander around the Streets of Stone Town, Zanzibar

    The Narrow Streets of Stone Town: With stunning architecture, narrow, labyrinth-like streets and excellent food; this is the perfect place to chill, explore and soak up the quintessential feel of a Swahili town.

    See More about Zanzibar
